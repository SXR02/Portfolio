Journey

My journey into UX/UI design began when I was working as Project Intern, where I learnt about the user experience, when working on student engagement platform. Without any key foundational knowledge of UX/UI, I went on to work with the developer to improve the aesthetics of the site, as well as improve the retention of student engaging with the platform. I realise the impact that intuitive and empathetic design can have enabling users to return to use the platform. The work I had undertook, both UX/UI help reduce the overall bounce back rate for new users, and existing users returning to use the platform throughout their education. It’s when I embarked on a software development bootcamp, where I encountered the terms UX/UI. I found myself focusing on design for a further two more project Santander, and BrumGo. Here, I worked with developers, closely creating designs and feeding back to the team. I enjoyed the process of brainstorming, creating, iteration, and building. This took me to keep developing my knowledge, using Coursa, Google UX modules, building a further project. When the opportunity presented itself, I couldn’t wait to start the School of UX bootcamp, working on a further 4 projects which further expanded my knowledge on user-centred, design principles, wireframing, and prototyping using tools like Figma. Since then, I have continued, to read, watch and add various layers to my knowledge.
